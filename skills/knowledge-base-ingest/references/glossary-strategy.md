# Glossary Strategy for Long-Form Ingestion

## Goal
Turn repeated concepts, terms, names, and framework labels into reusable glossary or concept-page candidates instead of leaving them buried inside chapters.

## Good glossary candidates
- repeated technical terms
- stable framework names
- domain-specific concepts
- acronyms that appear across multiple chapters
- named methods, protocols, or models

## Weak glossary candidates
- one-off narrative phrases
- chapter-local wording that does not generalize
- generic words with no domain meaning
- raw sentence fragments

## Recommended handling

### Option A: One glossary page
Use when:
- the source is book-like
- the terms are mostly definitional
- the vault does not need one page per term

### Option B: Promote selected terms into topic pages
Use when:
- a term has enough substance for its own page
- it connects multiple chapters
- it is likely to be linked from other parts of the vault

## Practical rule
Do not blindly create one page per extracted term.
Start from:
- glossary candidates list
- repeated terms across multiple chunks
- terms that deserve backlinks from several pages
